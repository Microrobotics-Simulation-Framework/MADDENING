**GPU-dependent FVM failures — root cause found and fixed (`MIME/feat/fvm-float64-audit`):**

The dtype hypothesis was only partly right.  The actual root cause is
**explicit-convection CFL violation**, not a dtype-plumbing bug.  The
PISO solver in `mime/nodes/environment/fvm/piso.py` is structurally
dtype-aware (every `jnp.zeros`/`jnp.array` already uses
`mesh.V.dtype`).  Float32 GPU reduction-order noise was incidentally
acting as numerical viscosity, damping an unstable convection mode
that the tests' chosen `dt` was provoking; the result was a stable
but heavily contaminated answer.  Float64 strips the artificial
damping and the scheme blows to ±inf.

| Test | Original `dt` | CFL | Float32 err | Float64 err | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `taylor_green` | 0.4·dx (CFL≈0.4 — already CFL-OK) | ✓ | 21.65% | clean fix via x64 | `dtype=jnp.float64` + `jax_enable_x64=True` |
| `womersley_wo7` | T/80 | 7.7 | 26.11% | NaN by step 75 | drop `dt` to T/640 (CFL≈0.96) + float64 |
| `ibm_poiseuille_2d` | 1.0 | 7.7 | 42.84% | NaN | drop `dt` to 0.1 (CFL≈0.75) + float64 |

The Taylor-Green case is the one the original peer diagnosis nailed
exactly: pure-central periodic flow at CFL≈0.4 *is* purely a
reduction-order-noise vs float32 story, and the fix is just to flip
the dtype.  The other two looked superficially similar but were
actually CFL violations masquerading as precision artefacts —
shrinking `dt` is the load-bearing change there; the dtype change is
required only because the previous "stability" was floating-point
artefact.

After the fix (MIME `22fed4f` + `ced7746`):
- Womersley amplitude error: 26.11% → **0.020%** (well under 2% bar)
- IBM Poiseuille profile error: 42.84% → **1.95%** (under 3% bar)
- Taylor-Green energy error: 21.65% → **<5%** (passes)

Full MIME suite on RTX A2000 + JAX 0.5.3 cuda12: 625 passed, 50
skipped, 51 deselected, 1 failed (lid_cavity — missing
`tmp/FVM/ghia-table1.json`, relocated to
`tests/verification/fixtures/` on the parallel
`feat/halo-width-migration` PR, so will resolve when that lands).

**Generalisable framing** for future FVM verification work: the
"implicit diffusion makes the time step unconditionally stable"
docstring shorthand has been hiding the explicit-convection CFL
constraint.  Tests must either honour `u_max · dt / dx ≲ 1`
explicitly or use an implicit/SL convection scheme.  Document the
CFL constraint in `piso.py` and `simple.py` docstrings.
